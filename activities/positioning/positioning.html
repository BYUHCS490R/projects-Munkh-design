Normal flow is the default way that browsers lay out HTML elements on a page.
Float moves an element to the left or right within its container, allowing other content to wrap around it.
The element is positioned relative to its normal position in the flow. The element is positioned relative to its closest positioned ancestor. The element is positioned relative to the browser window and stays in the same place even when the page is scrolled.
